While technology has advanced in many areas to improve accessibility, fraudsters still exploit the inability of blind or partially sighted individuals to verify currency notes easily. This exploitation not only causes financial loss but also creates emotional distress and erodes trust in everyday social interactions.

The Challenge Faced by the Visually Impaired

Currency identification is one of the major hurdles for people with visual disabilities. Many depend on trust when handling transactions in public places such as markets, shops, and transport services. Unfortunately, fraudsters take advantage of this trust by handing over lower denomination notes or even counterfeit money. For example, if a visually impaired person gives a high-value note, a dishonest individual may return incorrect change, cheating them without their knowledge. Since they cannot visually confirm the currency, the fraud often goes unnoticed until much later.
